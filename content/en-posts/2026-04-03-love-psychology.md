## Love Has a Style

Romance feels spontaneous, but beneath every relationship lies a **psychological pattern**. Psychology calls this your **Attachment Style** — a framework first proposed by British psychologist John Bowlby.

The theory suggests that how you bonded with caregivers in childhood shapes how you love as an adult.

---

## The 4 Attachment Styles

### 1. Secure
The healthiest type, representing about 55% of people. Secure individuals trust both themselves and their partners, balancing closeness with independence naturally.

**Mindset:** "I deserve love, and I trust you."

### 2. Anxious
Highly sensitive to a partner's emotions and often afraid of being abandoned. Frequent reassurance-seeking, anxiety when messages go unanswered.

**Mindset:** "What if they leave me? Am I not enough?"

### 3. Avoidant
Values independence strongly and pulls back as intimacy deepens. Struggles with emotional expression and may prioritize work or hobbies over connection.

**Mindset:** "Too much closeness suffocates me. I need my space."

### 4. Disorganized
Craves intimacy but fears it simultaneously. Unpredictable emotional swings and relationship patterns — the most complex type.

**Mindset:** "I want to stay but I want to run."

## MBTI and Love Patterns

Your MBTI type also shapes how you love:

- **E types**: Expressive and social — they show love openly and value shared experiences
- **I types**: Deep, private connection — meaningful one-on-one time matters most
- **F types**: Prioritize emotional resonance and empathy
- **T types**: Favor logical communication and problem-solving together
